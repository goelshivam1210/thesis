\chapter{Background}

This chapter introduces the formal frameworks used throughout the dissertation. The goal is not to provide a survey of the literature, but to establish the mathematical models and notation required to describe the methods developed in subsequent chapters.

The dissertation builds on three paradigms for sequential decision making: symbolic planning, reinforcement learning, and hybrid planning and learning. These paradigms provide distinct but complementary models for representing and solving decision-making problems.


Section~\ref{sec:symbolic_planning} presents the formalism of symbolic planning, including representations of states, fluents, and operators. Section~\ref{sec:rl} presents reinforcement learning in the Markov Decision Process framework. Section~\ref{sec:hybrid} presents a formal model of hybrid planning and learning systems that integrates symbolic reasoning with execution in the environment.
