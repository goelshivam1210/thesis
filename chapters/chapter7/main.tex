\chapter{Conclusion}
\label{ch:conclusion}

This dissertation investigated the role of structured abstractions in enabling autonomous agents and robots to operate effectively in open-world environments. The central thesis is that while novelty---mismatch between an agent's internal models and the environment---is unavoidable in real-world deployment, the structure of an agent's representations determines whether this mismatch leads to brittle failure or can be managed through efficient adaptation and generalizable behavior. We developed this thesis through three complementary contributions, summarized below, and conclude with a discussion of future directions.
